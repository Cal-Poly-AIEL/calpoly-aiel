About

The Cal Poly AI Ethics Lab (AI/EL) seeks to promote interdisciplinary conversations that investigate the distinctive ethical, legal, and social challenges presented by emerging AI technologies by:

  • Connecting students with faculty and research opportunities in these fields.
  • Developing papers and projects investigating these topics and pursuing grant and conference opportunities.
  • Bridging the gap between ethical frameworks, policy, and technical implementation in AI.

Paper Review and Discussions

During our regular meetings, we select topical papers or current events related to the ethical and societal implications of emerging AI technologies and use these resources as a starting place for conversation. Within these discussions, we aim to identify questions and solutions that we would like to explore further in the form of research papers or technical projects.

Faculty and Guest Speakers, Research

At AI/EL, we hope to build a network across disciplines at Cal Poly of students and faculty working to further investigate AI ethics. We aim to develop an ethically and technically literate student membership so that when research opportunities arise, we are ready to meet the challenge. Throughout the year, we also collaborate with faculty to host guest presentations where members can learn more about ethical frameworks, public policy, how AI is used in various disciplines, and the technical implementation of AI.

Grant Research, Conference Papers, and Project Opportunities

For the problems the lab chooses to explore in depth, our goal is to provide the resources and guidance needed for students to produce meaningful research within the field of AI ethics. We believe that research in this field must be view both from a holistic and interdisciplinary lens investigating problems from ethical, regulatory, and technical perspectives.
